Open HWP Files on iPhone: 3 Fast, Free Methods [2026]

Can't open an HWP file on your iPhone? Here are 3 free methods that work in 2026 — no PC, no conversion, no subscription required.

You can open an HWP file on iPhone right now — for free — using a document viewer app like OpenDocs. No PC required, no file conversion, no email-to-yourself workarounds.

If you've ever received a Korean government form, a school assignment, or a work document ending in .hwp and had no idea how to read it on your iPhone, you're not alone. HWP is the native format of Hangul Word Processor (한글), and Apple's built-in apps simply don't support it. iPhones don't ship with any software that can decode the format natively.

But that doesn't mean you're stuck. In 2026, there are at least three practical ways to open HWP files on iOS — and the fastest one takes under two minutes.

Why HWP Files Won't Open on iPhone by Default

HWP is a proprietary format developed by Hancom, the South Korean software company behind the Hangul Word Processor suite. It's been the standard document format for Korean government agencies, schools, and businesses for decades.

The problem for iPhone users is straightforward:

  • Apple's Files app doesn't support HWP
  • Microsoft Word on iOS cannot open HWP files
  • Google Docs also lacks native HWP support
  • Even desktop Mac users need a third-party solution

This leaves a lot of people — especially Korean expats, international students, and anyone dealing with Korean administrative paperwork — stranded every time an .hwp file lands in their inbox.

The good news: the workarounds are simple, and the best ones are completely free.

Method 1: Use a Free HWP Viewer App (Fastest Option)

The most direct solution is downloading a dedicated document viewer that supports the HWP format natively. OpenDocs (available free on iOS and Android) is one of the cleanest options available in 2026.

How to open an HWP file with OpenDocs

  1. Download OpenDocs from the App Store (free, no account needed)
  2. Locate your HWP file — it might be in your Files app, an email attachment, or a messaging app like KakaoTalk
  3. Tap the file and use the iOS Share Sheet (the box-with-arrow icon)
  4. Select "Open in OpenDocs" from the share options
  5. The file opens immediately, rendered cleanly on your screen

OpenDocs supports HWP, PDF, DOCX, XLSX, and more — so it doubles as a general document viewer for everything else in your workflow too.

Why this method works best

  • No internet connection required after the app is installed
  • No file size limits (unlike most web-based converters)
  • No account, no login, no subscription — just install and open
  • Works with files stored locally, in iCloud Drive, Google Drive, or Dropbox

If you're regularly dealing with HWP files — for work, school, or government forms — this is the method worth setting up once and forgetting about.

Method 2: Convert HWP to PDF Online Before Opening

If you only need to view an HWP file occasionally and don't want to install an extra app, converting the file to PDF first is a reasonable option.

Steps for online conversion

  1. On your iPhone, open Safari and search for an HWP-to-PDF converter (several free options exist)
  2. Upload your .hwp file from your Files app
  3. Download the converted PDF
  4. Open it with any PDF viewer — including Apple's built-in Books app or Files preview

Limitations to keep in mind

For sensitive documents — tax forms, medical records, legal paperwork — uploading to a third-party server is worth thinking twice about. A local app keeps everything on your device.

Method 3: Open HWP Files via Cloud Storage (Google Drive Workaround)

Google Drive added limited HWP viewing support in recent years. If someone shares an HWP file with you via Google Drive, you may be able to preview it directly in the Drive app without downloading anything.

How to try the Google Drive method

  1. Open the Google Drive iOS app
  2. Navigate to the shared HWP file
  3. Tap the file to open the preview
  4. If Drive supports the file, it renders in-app — otherwise, you'll see a "Can't preview" error

Why this isn't always reliable

Google Drive's HWP support is inconsistent. Complex documents — those with tables, images, headers, or special Korean fonts — often fail to render correctly or don't open at all. This method works best for simple, text-heavy HWP files.

For anything more complex, fall back to Method 1.

What to Do When an HWP File Still Won't Open

If you've tried the steps above and the file still isn't opening correctly, here are the most common causes and fixes:

The file is corrupted or incomplete → Ask the sender to re-send the file. HWP files can get corrupted during some messaging apps' compression.

The file was sent as a link, not an attachment → Some Korean portals (like government websites) require a logged-in session to download. Try opening the download link in Safari on iPhone, then use the share sheet to pass it to OpenDocs.

The file is an older HWP format (HWP 2.x or 3.x) → Very old Hangul documents use legacy formats that even modern viewers sometimes struggle with. Try converting to PDF first using Method 2.

The app didn't register as a share target → After installing OpenDocs, try restarting your iPhone. iOS sometimes needs a reboot to refresh the list of apps available in the share sheet.

Managing HWP Files on iPhone Long-Term

Once you've got a working method in place, a few habits make managing HWP files much smoother on iOS:

  • Save HWP files to iCloud Drive so they're accessible across all your Apple devices
  • Tag them in Files with a color label so they're easy to find later
  • Set OpenDocs as your default handler — after using it a few times via share sheet, iOS will start suggesting it automatically when you tap an HWP file
  • Keep a PDF copy of important HWP documents (government forms, contracts) so you can access them even if the viewer app has an issue in the future

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it free to open HWP files on iPhone? Yes. OpenDocs is completely free on iOS with no in-app purchases required for basic viewing. Online converters also offer free tiers, though with file size caps.

Can I edit HWP files on iPhone, not just view them? Editing HWP on iOS is significantly harder than viewing. The official Hancom Office app (한컴오피스) supports editing but requires a subscription. For most users, viewing and sharing as PDF covers the practical need.

Does this work on iPad too? Yes. Any method that works on iPhone works identically on iPad. The larger screen actually makes reading HWP documents — especially those with tables and columns — more comfortable.

What if my HWP file has images or complex layouts? OpenDocs handles mixed-content HWP files (text + images + tables) well. Online converters vary more — if the layout looks wrong after conversion, try a different converter or use the app directly.

Is my document safe when I open it in OpenDocs? OpenDocs processes files locally on your device. Nothing is uploaded to a server. This makes it a solid choice for sensitive documents.
