How to Open HWP Files on iPhone — 3 Easy Methods

Can't open an HWP file on your iPhone? Here are 3 free methods that actually work in 2026, including a one-tap app solution.

How to Open HWP Files on iPhone — 3 Easy Methods

You can open HWP files on your iPhone right now using a free document viewer app like OpenDocs — no PC required, no conversion needed, just tap and read.

HWP is South Korea's dominant word processor format, developed by Hancom. If you've ever received an HWP file from a Korean school, government office, or company and found yourself staring at a blank screen on your iPhone, you're not alone. Apple's built-in apps don't support the format, and most Western document apps ignore it entirely.

But the fix is simpler than you think.

Why Your iPhone Can't Open HWP Files by Default

Apple's Files app and Quick Look previewer support a wide range of formats — PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets — but HWP is not on that list. The format is proprietary to Hancom and requires a dedicated parser to render correctly.

This means:

  • Tapping an HWP file in your email often shows a blank preview or an error
  • Files app may display it as an unknown file type
  • Sharing to other apps doesn't help unless the target app explicitly supports HWP

The good news is that the iOS ecosystem has matured, and today there are reliable, free options for reading HWP on iPhone.

Method 1 — Use a Free HWP Viewer App (Fastest Option)

This is the most straightforward path. A dedicated document viewer app that supports HWP will handle everything natively, right on your iPhone.

OpenDocs is one of the few free iOS apps that supports HWP alongside PDF, DOCX, and XLSX — which means you don't need a different app for each file type.

How to open an HWP file with OpenDocs

  1. Download OpenDocs from the App Store (free)
  2. Open the HWP file from wherever it lives — Mail, iMessage, Files, KakaoTalk
  3. Tap the Share icon → select Open in OpenDocs
  4. The file opens instantly, fully rendered with Korean text, tables, and images intact

That's it. No account required, no upload to a cloud server, no conversion step.

This method works offline and keeps your document private — especially important when the file contains personal or official information.

Method 2 — Convert HWP to PDF First (PC Required)

If you have access to a Windows PC with Hancom Office installed, you can save the HWP file as a PDF and then transfer it to your iPhone.

Steps

  1. Open the HWP file in Hancom Word (한글) on Windows
  2. Go to File → Save As → choose PDF
  3. Transfer the PDF to your iPhone via AirDrop, iCloud Drive, or email
  4. Open the PDF with Files app or any PDF viewer

Pros: Perfect rendering since the conversion happens inside the official app.
Cons: Requires a Windows PC, takes extra steps, and doesn't work on the go.

This method is fine as a one-off workaround, but it's clearly not practical if you regularly deal with HWP files from your phone.

Method 3 — Use an Online HWP Converter

Several web-based services let you upload an HWP file and download a converted PDF or DOCX in return.

Popular options include:

  • Convertio (convertio.co)
  • CloudConvert (cloudconvert.com)
  • Online2PDF (online2pdf.com)

How it works

  1. Open Safari on your iPhone
  2. Go to one of the converter sites above
  3. Upload your HWP file
  4. Download the converted PDF

Important caveats

  • Your file is uploaded to a third-party server — avoid this with sensitive documents
  • Formatting may break on complex HWP files (tables, frames, footnotes)
  • Requires a stable internet connection
  • Some converters have file size limits on free plans

This method is better than nothing, but for regular use it adds friction and raises privacy concerns.

Tips for Working with HWP Files on iPhone

A few things worth knowing once you have a viewer set up:

Check the HWP version
Older HWP files (pre-2010) use a different internal format than modern ones. Most viewers handle both, but if a file looks off, it may be a legacy version.

Text extraction may vary
If an HWP file contains scanned images instead of real text, no viewer can make the text selectable — that's an OCR limitation, not a viewer problem.

Keep a PDF copy for sharing
When sending a document to someone outside Korea, export or print-to-PDF before sending. Most people outside Korea can't open HWP files natively either.

iCloud Drive works smoothly
If your HWP files live in iCloud Drive, most viewer apps can connect directly without needing to manually share the file each time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there an official Hancom app for iPhone?

Hancom has released iOS apps in the past, but availability and free-tier limitations vary by region. As of 2026, users outside Korea often find third-party viewers more accessible for read-only use.

Can I edit HWP files on iPhone?

Full HWP editing on iOS is limited. Most apps offer read-only viewing. If you need to make edits, the practical workaround is converting to DOCX, editing in Microsoft Word or Pages, and then asking the recipient to re-save in HWP if needed.

Does OpenDocs support HWP files with images and tables?

Yes. OpenDocs renders HWP files including embedded images, tables, and Korean typography. Complex layouts may have minor rendering differences compared to the desktop app, but content is fully readable.

What about HWP files sent over KakaoTalk?

KakaoTalk on iOS lets you tap and hold an attachment to share it. Choose Open in OpenDocs (or your preferred viewer) from the share sheet. The file opens directly without saving it separately first.
